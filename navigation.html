    Introduction

    To get started, a GPS stands for Global Positioning System. It is an electronic version of a map. As of February 2012, 74% of smartphone users use location-based apps. This was 19% more users than in May 2011. This means that GPS are getting more popular while the usage of paper maps are decreasing. To use a GPS is more simple as you only need type the destination you want to go to and you will get a voice helper. Depending on the GPS you get, you can get more features.

    Function of the GPS

    The job involves a device (GPS) and a satellite. To make a GPS work, you need to input the destination you want to go to and that's it. An automated voice does the rest. You just follow the voice and eventually, you will reach your destination. There are other functions on the GPS such as and electronic compass and scale. The GPS also gives you written directions and gives you the speed you are going at. They also tell you what time you will reach your destination.

    Impacts of GPS

    On a GPS, there are many features. You can book at a restaurant and or call from it. Since you are mainly looking at the screen, you won't have any social conversations with other people. The only contact with people would be at the place you are going to. GPS costs can vary from $100-$300. You might think this is a lot, but it cost $12 billion to put a satellite into space. Some environmental impacts could be the satellite going into space. In many years, it could become space junk.

    Conclusion

    In conclusion, I will have to say that the GPS is a good choice. The GPS may cost a lot, but the cost is a one-time payment. For a paper map, we will have to keep cutting our trees to get it. Also, the GPS has many features, such as: Bluetooth, Calling, Recents etc. On a paper map, you will forget which way you were holding the paper or what direction you were going in. The GPS also allows you to book at restaurants. Overall, the GPS is much more advanced and more user-friendly.
